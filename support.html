Support

Help with the AI calendar app for iPhone and iPad.

Quick answers

  • Why does the app work without AI?

    The free tier (calendar viewing, ritual tracking, protected blocks, widgets, Weekly Review in data-only mode) doesn't require AI. AI features are an opt-in unlock.

  • What's a "ritual"?

    A daily or weekly habit that lives alongside your calendar — like meditation, gym, or a daily review. Streaks and a 7-day completion strip make consistency visible. Rituals show up in widgets and the morning brief.

  • What's a "protected block"?

    Recurring time you've marked as committed (Focus Work, Sleep, Family, etc.). When AI scheduling suggests reschedules or finds open slots, it respects these blocks.

  • Will Bract work with my Google Calendar or Outlook?

    Yes, via Apple Calendar. If your Google or Outlook account is added in iOS Settings → Calendars, Bract reads it through EventKit just like Apple Calendar does — no separate setup in Bract.

  • How do I set up AI features?

    Open Settings → AI Provider, choose Anthropic, OpenAI, or Google, and paste an API key from your provider's developer console.

  • What does AI cost?

    A one-time in-app unlock turns on AI features. Provider compute (Claude, GPT, Gemini) is billed directly by your chosen provider — typically under $1 per month for regular use.

  • Why does Bract ask for calendar permission?

    Bract reads your existing Apple calendars to display events and detect scheduling conflicts. Your data stays on device unless you explicitly use an AI feature (which sends only event titles and times).

  • Can I switch AI providers?

    Yes. One unlock works with all three. Change provider any time in Settings; your stored keys for other providers are preserved.

Reporting issues

If something is broken, please email support@bract.app.

Every message reaches a real human. Bract is built by one developer — replies are personal, not templated.

When reporting an issue, please include:

  • Your device and OS version (e.g., iPhone 16 Pro on iOS 26)
  • What you were trying to do
  • What happened instead
  • If AI-related: which provider you have configured (Anthropic / OpenAI / Google)

Privacy

For data-handling details, see the privacy policy. Short version: calendar data stays on your device; AI features send only event titles and times to your chosen provider; no tracking, no analytics, no data selling.
