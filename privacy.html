Privacy Policy

Effective: 2026-05-22

In short

  • No central database Bract has no server. We hold no user data.
  • Calendar data stays on your device.
  • AI requests event titles and times only, sent directly from your device to the provider you chose.
  • Tracking none. No analytics. No advertising identifiers.
  • Selling nothing to sell — no database of users exists.

Bract v1.0 ships on iPhone.

Bract is the publisher and data controller for the limited app data described below. For privacy questions and to exercise your rights under applicable law, contact support@bract.app.

1. Data Access

Bract accesses your calendar and reminder data locally via Apple's EventKit framework. This data stays on your device and is never uploaded to external servers except as described below.

2. AI Features & Data Sent to Third Parties

When you use AI features (natural-language scheduling, Weekly Review), Bract sends only event titles and times to your chosen AI provider's API. You pick the provider in Settings; Bract supports:

  • Anthropic — sends to api.anthropic.com (Claude). Privacy policy: anthropic.com/legal/privacy
  • OpenAI — sends to api.openai.com (GPT). Privacy policy: openai.com/policies/privacy-policy
  • Google — sends to generativelanguage.googleapis.com (Gemini). Privacy policy: policies.google.com/privacy

No event notes, attendees, locations, or attachments are ever sent. Bract sends only the title and start/end time of relevant events.

All AI requests use your personal API key, which you provide and manage with the chosen provider. Bract does not have its own API key, does not proxy your requests, and does not retain a copy of what's sent.

When you use AI features, you direct Bract to send your request to a provider you selected, using your own API key. Under data-protection law, you are the controller of these requests; the provider is your processor. Bract is a technical conduit and is not party to your relationship with the provider.

Your AI provider may process your data outside your country, including in the United States. Bract is not a party to those transfers; your provider's privacy policy governs them.

3. Data Storage

Each provider's API key is stored independently in the device's secure Keychain, protected by biometric authentication (Face ID, Touch ID, or device passcode). Switching providers in Settings does not reveal or move your stored keys. All other app data is stored locally using Apple's SwiftData framework on encrypted storage.

4. No Tracking or Analytics

Bract does not track you across apps or websites. Bract does not collect analytics, crash reports, or usage data. Bract does not use advertising identifiers.

Bract does not use Apple's App Tracking Transparency identifiers (IDFA) or any cross-app/cross-site tracking technology.

5. No Data Selling

Bract has nothing to sell because we maintain no central database of users. We will not develop one for advertising or marketing purposes without giving you notice and the opportunity to opt out.

6. Your Control

Bract maintains no central database, no user accounts, and no server-side data store. All control happens on your device:

  • Delete each provider's API key at any time in Settings.
  • Revoke calendar access in iOS Settings.
  • Remove all locally stored data by deleting the app.

7. Your Rights

Bract is built so that you control all your data without needing to ask us. Because we hold no central database, most data-protection rights you may have (access, correction, deletion, portability, restriction) are exercised by you directly:

  • To view, edit, or delete your calendar data: use the Apple Calendar app or iOS Settings → Calendars. Your data lives there, not with Bract.
  • To delete locally stored Bract data (rituals, protected blocks, settings): delete the app from your device.
  • To delete API keys you have stored: open Settings in Bract and remove each provider's key, or delete the app.
  • To request deletion of data you may have sent to an AI provider: contact your chosen provider directly using the privacy-policy links in section 2 above. Bract has no copy of those requests.

If you live in a jurisdiction with a data-protection supervisory authority (such as an EU/EEA Data Protection Authority or the California Privacy Protection Agency), you have the right to lodge a complaint with them. For questions about how Bract handles data, email support@bract.app.

8. California Residents

Under the CCPA/CPRA, California residents have rights including the right to know, delete, correct, and opt out of sale or sharing. Bract's no-central-database architecture means most of these rights are moot for data we touch:

  • Right to know: Section 2 above tells you exactly what data leaves your device (event titles and times only, only when you trigger an AI feature) and where it goes (the AI provider you chose).
  • Right to delete: We hold no database of your data, so there is nothing on Bract's end to delete. To delete data your AI provider may have logged, contact them directly.
  • Right to opt out of sale or sharing: Bract does not sell or share personal information for cross-context behavioral advertising, and we will not without giving you notice.

To raise any of these matters with us, email support@bract.app.

9. Children's Privacy

Bract is not directed to children under 13, and we do not knowingly accept personal data from them. The 4+ App Store age rating reflects content suitability, not a children's-app designation. Because Bract maintains no central database of users, we cannot identify a child's account on our end. A parent can remove all Bract-related data by deleting the app from the device and revoking any provider API key in iOS Settings. If you believe a child has used Bract and need help removing data from a third-party AI provider, email support@bract.app and we will point you to the right contact.

10. Contact & Changes

For questions about this privacy policy, email support@bract.app. We will notify you of material changes via an in-app notice before they take effect.
