What's next

What's in the works, what's further out, and a few things we've decided not to build.

Shipping in v1.0

What you get on day one.

  • Protected blocks.

    Mark recurring time as Focus, Sleep, Family, or your own. AI suggestions and rescheduling respect these blocks.

  • Rituals with streaks.

    Daily and weekly habits in the calendar. A 7-day strip and streak count make consistency visible.

  • Natural-language scheduling.

    Describe an event in your own words. Bring your own AI provider key — Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, or Google Gemini.

  • Weekly Review.

    What got done, what slipped, where your week went. Optional AI summary; data-only mode works without a key.

  • Widgets.

    Lock Screen and Home Screen — rituals, next event, morning brief. Your day visible before you open the app.

  • Morning Brief.

    A single notification with today's outlook — events, rituals, anything that needs attention.

What's next

Landing in the first updates after launch.

  • Real-world bug fixes.

    Whatever the first weeks of installs surface. Reply to support@bract.app and it goes onto the list.

  • Polish on natural-language scheduling.

    The AI handles most requests well. Unusual phrasing occasionally misfires — every report helps.

  • More AI provider choice.

    Three providers ship at launch — Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini. As the bring-your-own-key ecosystem widens, more will follow.

On our list

Actively being built. Order may shift as work clarifies.

  • Apple Watch companion.

    A glanceable Bract on your wrist — today's rituals, what's next, a tap to mark them done. Complications for the watch face so the next thing is always there, without reaching for your phone.

  • A weekly intentions list.

    Things you want to do this week, without locking them to a time. Lower-stakes than putting everything on the calendar; higher-stakes than a to-do list that nobody reads on Wednesday.

  • Deeper Weekly Review.

    The Review tab already shows what completed and what slipped. Next: what changed overnight, what's at risk this week, where the AI moved things to protect your focus.

  • Block visual nesting.

    A Deep Work block with a meeting inside it should look like the meeting belongs to the block. Small visual change, big mental-model shift.

Where we're headed

Directions, not commitments.

  • Bract notices what you actually do.

    You accept morning focus slots and always push Friday meetings to Thursday. Over time, Bract learns those patterns and factors them in. No preferences screen needed. This is the furthest-out item on the list.

  • Direct calendar integration for Google and Microsoft.

    Today Bract reads these through iOS Settings — add your Google or Outlook account there and they show up automatically. A future update lets you connect them directly inside Bract for richer fields and faster sync, without sending your data to a Bract server. Same model as your AI key: your credentials stay in your iPhone Keychain, all calls go directly between your iPhone and the provider.

  • Proactive block defense.

    A meeting lands on your Deep Work block. The plan: Bract moves the block to the next slot that works and shows a single "moved it — undo?" prompt. Not shipped yet — getting this right matters more than getting it out.

  • Travel-aware scheduling.

    Travel-aware scheduling — "I'm in Lisbon Wed–Sun, plan around that" — is on the list. Time zones and how the AI explains date-boundary decisions need careful work before it ships.

  • Energy and sleep-aware scheduling.

    Planned: Bract will use signals from Apple Health — sleep data, resting heart rate — to flag low-energy days in scheduling suggestions. Your Health data stays on iPhone; the AI sees only a label like "low-energy day."

What we're not doing

Things we've thought about and decided against.

  • No team or shared calendars.

    Bract is for one person's week. Team scheduling is a different product. iCloud, Google, and Outlook already handle shared calendars; Bract reads them through Apple's accounts integration.

  • No web app, no sync server, no account.

    Your data lives on your device, in Apple's calendar database. There is no Bract cloud — and there won't be one.

  • No tracking, no analytics, no third-party SDKs.

    No tracking. Not now, not in a future update — the app has no mechanism for it.

  • No autonomous AI.

    Bract suggests; you confirm. The AI never moves an event without you tapping. We don't want to ship surprises into your calendar — that's not the relationship we want with your time.

  • No voice assistant.

    Dictation works in every iOS text field — including Bract's natural-language box. A separate voice flow would add a microphone permission, a recording UI, and a transcription dependency we don't need.

This is a direction, not a schedule. Items may move, change shape, or get dropped. If something here is the reason you'd buy Bract, wait until it ships.
