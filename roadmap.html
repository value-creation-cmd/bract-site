What's next

What we're working on, what's further out, and what we've decided not to build. No dates.

What's next

Landing in the first updates after launch.

  • Real-world bug fixes.

    Whatever the first weeks of installs surface. Reply to support@bract.app and it goes onto the list.

  • Polish on natural-language scheduling.

    The AI is good. Some phrasings still throw it. Each one we hear about makes the next person's first try smoother.

  • More AI provider choice.

    Three providers ship at launch — Anthropic Claude, OpenAI GPT, Google Gemini. As the bring-your-own-key ecosystem widens, more will follow.

On our list

Actively being built. Order may shift as work clarifies.

  • Bract for Mac.

    The codebase is universal — Mac needs its own design pass before it ships. We want to do it properly, not port it sloppily.

  • A weekly intentions list.

    Things you want to do this week, without locking them to a time. Lower-stakes than putting everything on the calendar; higher-stakes than a to-do list that nobody reads on Wednesday.

  • Deeper Weekly Review.

    The Review tab already shows what completed and what slipped. Next: what changed overnight, what's at risk this week, where the AI moved things to protect your focus.

  • Block visual nesting.

    A Deep Work block with a meeting inside it should look like the meeting belongs to the block. Small visual change, big mental-model shift.

Where we're headed

Directions, not commitments.

  • Bract notices what you actually do.

    You accept morning focus slots, decline late-afternoon ones, always shift Friday meetings to Thursday. No preferences screen — the app gets progressively righter without asking. This is the longest-term direction.

  • Proactive block defense.

    A meeting lands on your Deep Work block. Bract quietly moves the block to the next slot that works and shows you a single "moved it — undo?" toast. You stop having to think about protecting your focus time.

  • Travel-aware scheduling.

    Hooks for "I'm in Lisbon Wed–Sun, plan around that" already exist in the code. Turning it on takes a careful pass on time zones and how the AI explains its choices.

What we're not doing

These are deliberate choices, not omissions.

  • No team or shared calendars.

    Bract is for one person's week. Team scheduling is a different product. iCloud, Google, and Outlook already handle shared calendars; Bract reads them through Apple's accounts integration.

  • No web app, no sync server, no account.

    Your data lives on your device, in Apple's calendar database. There is no Bract cloud — and there won't be one. We'd rather build the best native app than a worse cross-platform one.

  • No tracking, no analytics, no third-party SDKs.

    Permanent commitment, not "currently." This is the architecture, not a configuration.

  • No autonomous AI.

    Bract suggests; you confirm. The AI never moves an event without you tapping. We don't want to ship surprises into your calendar — that's not the relationship we want with your time.

  • No voice assistant.

    Dictation works in every iOS text field — including Bract's natural-language box. A separate voice flow would add a microphone permission, a recording UI, and a transcription dependency we don't need.

This page describes directions we're working in, not a release schedule. We don't publish dates because indie software ships when it's right, not when a roadmap says so. Items here may move, change shape, or get dropped if we learn they aren't useful — and we'd rather tell you that honestly than ship something half-built to hit a quarter. If something here is the reason you'd buy Bract, please wait until it ships before you do.
